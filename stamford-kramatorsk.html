A Symbolic Date for an Important Friendship

On April 6, 2023, the cities of Stamford, Connecticut, USA and Kramatorsk, Donetsk, Ukraine signed a memorandum formally establishing a relationship of friendship and support. Mayor Caroline Simmons of Stamford and Mayor Oleksandr Honcharenko of Kramatorsk exchanged words of hope for the future of Kramatorsk and the friendship between their two cities.

This date holds deep significance. Exactly one year prior, on April 6, 2022, a missile strike on the Kramatorsk railway station killed over 60 civilians and wounded more than 100 individuals in the early days of the war. This attack was widely condemned as an act of terrorism and a war crime. One year later, the people of Stamford agreed to rally together in support of Kramatorsk to bring much-needed aid to this vital city in eastern Ukraine.

"Thank you to the generous citizens of Stamford for supporting our people during our hour of greatest need."

— Oleksandr Honcharenko, Mayor of Kramatorsk
April 6, 2023

Two Cities, One Mission

🇺🇦 Kramatorsk, Ukraine

Population: ~150,000
Region: Donetsk Oblast
Founded: 1868

Kramatorsk is a major industrial city in eastern Ukraine, known for its machine-building industry and rich cultural heritage. Despite facing significant challenges due to its proximity to the conflict zone, the city remains resilient. Its residents continue to rebuild, support one another, and maintain hope for a peaceful future.

🇺🇸 Stamford, Connecticut

Population: ~135,000
State: Connecticut
Founded: 1641

Stamford is a thriving city on Connecticut's Gold Coast, home to numerous Fortune 500 companies and a vibrant, diverse community. The city has embraced its role as a sister city to Kramatorsk with enthusiasm and generosity, demonstrating the power of community-driven humanitarian action.
