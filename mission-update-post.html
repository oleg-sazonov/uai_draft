Winter Relief: 15 Tons of Aid Delivered to Kramatorsk

Field Mission January 15, 2026 📍 Kramatorsk, Ukraine 🤝 Stamford–Kramatorsk Partnership

Our team successfully completed another humanitarian mission, bringing essential supplies to families in need

On January 15, 2026, our team completed a successful humanitarian mission to Kramatorsk, delivering over 15 tons of essential aid to families affected by the ongoing conflict. This mission was made possible through the generous support of our sister city, Stamford, Connecticut.

Mission Overview

The winter months bring additional challenges to communities in eastern Ukraine. Temperatures regularly drop below freezing, and heating supplies are scarce. Our mission focused on providing:

  • Food supplies: Non-perishable food items for 800 families
  • Medical equipment: First aid supplies, medicines, and medical devices
  • Winter clothing: Warm coats, boots, blankets, and thermal underwear
  • Hygiene products: Essential hygiene items for men, women, and children
  • Children's supplies: School materials, toys, and nutritional supplements

Community Impact

The distribution took place at the Kramatorsk Community Center, where local volunteers helped organize and distribute the aid. Families lined up in the early morning, and by the end of the day, we had served over 800 families - approximately 3,200 individuals.

"The support from our American friends in Stamford means everything to us. It's not just the supplies - it's knowing that people across the ocean care about us and haven't forgotten about our struggle."

— Olena, mother of three from Kramatorsk

Partnership with Stamford

This mission was the result of months of collaboration between our teams in Kramatorsk and Stamford. The Stamford community rallied together, organizing fundraisers, donation drives, and awareness campaigns to support their sister city.

Mayor of Stamford, Caroline Simmons, shared her thoughts on the partnership: "The bond between Stamford and Kramatorsk represents the best of humanity. Our residents have opened their hearts and homes to support our Ukrainian friends, and we will continue to stand with them for as long as needed."

Looking Ahead

As we celebrate this successful mission, we're already planning our next trip. The needs remain substantial, and our commitment to supporting Kramatorsk and other Ukrainian communities continues. We invite you to join us in this effort by making a donation or volunteering for future missions.

Mission Team

This mission was carried out by a dedicated team of volunteers from both Stamford and Ukraine:

  • Michael Johnson - Team Leader
  • Sarah Williams - Logistics Coordinator
  • Andriy Kovalenko - Local Coordinator
  • Plus 15 additional volunteers from Stamford and Kramatorsk

We extend our heartfelt gratitude to everyone who contributed to making this mission a success.
